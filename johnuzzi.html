    Summary

    In this interview, Vietnam War veteran John Uzzi reflects on his experiences during and after the war. He discusses the challenges of transitioning back to civilian life, highlighting the support of his family, especially his wife. Uzzi emphasizes the value of military training and discipline, which he believes are often misunderstood by civilians. He recalls the deep bonds formed with fellow soldiers and the lingering memories of lost comrades. Uzzi also addresses the misdirected blame soldiers faced upon returning home and shares his struggles with PTSD. He notes the importance of having a plan when leaving the military and expresses skepticism towards modern media, advocating for a balanced perspective.
